# YourGbedu Design Direction

## Creative North Star

YourGbedu should feel like a warm editorial music gallery: personal, crafted, premium, and easy to move through. The product sells emotional trust, so the interface should make the user feel that real creative work is happening on their behalf.

The current visual direction is the warm "Sun-Drenched Gallery" version of the earlier design explorations. Preserve it over the darker cinematic variant unless the whole brand direction is intentionally revisited.

## Product Promise

Standard delivery is 48 hours. Frame the wait as active craftsmanship:

- "We'll have yours built and delivered in 48 hours."
- "Your custom song will be set up and in your inbox within 48 hours."
- "We configure everything around your story, and you'll be live in 48 hours."

Avoid passive phrases like "ready in 48 hours" or "available in 48 hours."

## Visual System

### Palette

- Canvas: warm ivory, not pure white.
- Text: deep ink/obsidian for authority and readability.
- Primary warmth: terracotta for action and emotional emphasis.
- Secondary warmth: mustard/gold for premium signals.
- Support: sage for calm status, reassurance, and secondary accents.

Use cyan only as a small spark if it returns to the system. It should not become a large surface color.

### Typography

- Display/headline: editorial serif, currently `Cormorant Garamond`.
- Body/UI: clean sans-serif, currently `DM Sans`.
- Pair large expressive headlines with compact labels or body copy.
- Keep letter spacing at 0 for large display copy; use tracked uppercase sparingly for labels.

### Layout

- Use generous whitespace and asymmetry to create a crafted, editorial rhythm.
- Avoid boxed-in page sections. Prefer full-width bands, tonal shifts, and unframed content.
- Cards should be individual tools or repeated items, not wrappers around whole page sections.
- Important product moments should be direct and scannable: choose clarity over decoration.

### Surfaces And Depth

- Prefer tonal layering over heavy shadows.
- Use soft ambient shadows only when an element genuinely floats.
- Avoid hard divider lines where spacing or background shifts can separate content.
- If an edge is needed for accessibility, use a subtle ghost border.

### Components

- Primary actions: deep ink or terracotta fills with cream text.
- Secondary actions: quiet warm surfaces with clear hover/focus states.
- Inputs: calm filled surfaces, visible labels, strong focus rings.
- Audio/player elements: minimal controls, warm progress states, no busy visualizers unless they add clear value.

## UX Principles

- The customer journey takes priority over admin polish.
- Every purchase step should reinforce that the song is being built, configured, or produced for the customer.
- Payment can stop at safe checkout states in local/dev testing.
- Empty, loading, and error states should sound helpful rather than technical.
- Admin screens should be dense and operational, but still use the same warm editorial tokens.

## Accessibility And Responsiveness

- Maintain visible focus states for every interactive control.
- Preserve readable contrast between terracotta/gold surfaces and text.
- Do not rely on color alone for status.
- Test customer-facing screens on mobile, tablet, and desktop before shipping visual changes.
- Respect reduced-motion preferences.
